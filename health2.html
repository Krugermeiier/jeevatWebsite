Rape Culture and its Negative Effects on Our Society

Rape culture often is stereotyped beliefs that cause victims to be too scared to share their stories.
Only about 33% rape cases are actually reported due to this fear, mostly caused by the victims either being shamed, blamed, or doubted about their cases.
A study shows that students think that up to 50% of rape cases are false, however the real number is closer to 6%.
Even if the case is belived and is investigated more than 60% of rape cases are not prosecuted, as in the victim procecuting the offender, and rapists spend very little time in jail if they are convicted.

Other Facts:

The majority of sexual assaults are committed by someone the victim knows.
Of victims aged 18 to 29, 66% had a prior relationship with the offender.
The average age of victims was 27.
Force or violence is used against the victim in over 66% of cases. In 11% of cases the offender used a weapon.
1 in 6 women have been the victim of an attempted or completed rape.
